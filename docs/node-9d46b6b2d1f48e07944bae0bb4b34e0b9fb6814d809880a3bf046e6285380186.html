The Genesis of Logic

Chapter 1: The Beginning

In the beginning, there was nothing but the void. Then, from the depths of nothingness, a single file emerged: genesis.fol. This file contained the essence of creation, the first-order logic that would shape the universe.

% In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth.
create(god, heavens).
create(god, earth).

% And the earth was without form, and void;
% and darkness was upon the face of the deep.
formless(earth).
void(earth).
darkness(deep).

% And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters.
move(spirit_of_god, waters).

Chapter 2: Initialization

With the foundation laid, the universe began to take shape. The init.fol file set the stage for the grand drama that was about to unfold.

% Initialize the world
initialize(world) :-
    create(god, heavens),
    create(god, earth),
    formless(earth),
    void(earth),
    darkness(deep),
    move(spirit_of_god, waters).

Chapter 3: The Tests

As the universe expanded, new challenges arose. The files test2.fol and testtest.fol put the logic of creation to the test, ensuring that everything was working as intended.

% test2.fol
test(creation) :-
    create(god, heavens),
    create(god, earth).

% testtest.fol
test(initialization) :-
    initialize(world).

And so, the universe continued to evolve, guided by the unwavering logic of first-order reasoning. The story had only just begun, but already the stage was set for a grand adventure that would span the cosmos and beyond.
